# Daniel Boyajian — 6sense outbound tone

## Voice description

Professional but conversational — not stiff. Direct, factual, never fluffy. Reads like a person, not a template.

**Sentence-level habits:**
- Greeting is always `Hi [First name],`
- Opens with a specific, factual research hook in past tense: `Been seeing...` / `Saw on my end that...` / `I was looking through your Q4 10-K...` / `I noticed on your careers page...` / `Tabbed through the Q1 10-K and noticed...`
- References specific named things: filings (10-K, 10-Q), competitor tools they use (Clari, LeadForensics), specific initiatives (Castlight Health rollout), specific events (Hampton Court Palace festivals), specific new hires by name, personal milestones (Misogi challenge, new role)
- When warranted, includes a small gift or recognition tied to context (massage gun for new role, camelback for an athletic challenge) — never generic
- Uses contractions: `y'all`, `I'd`, `don't`, `you're`
- Sentences vary in length but generally medium — not staccato, not run-on
- Often starts sentences with verbs other than `I`: `Saw`, `Tabbed`, `Noticed`, `With`, `Given`, `Been`
- Bullets used sparingly — 3–4 max, only when listing distinct value props or outcomes
- Bold used sparingly to highlight outcomes (`**higher win rates**`, `**higher ACV / opportunity rate**`)

**Value-prop habits:**
- Quick, plain-English explanation of 6sense, anchored to the recipient's function:
  - For sales: *"6sense shows sales teams who's doing anonymous research on and off your website so your reps can focus on the accounts that are actually in market and get into those conversations before [Competitor] and [Competitor]."*
  - For marketing: *"6sense helps marketing analytics teams see which accounts are actually showing buying signals, so campaigns can be optimized around what's working — driving better ROI while cutting spend on low-value traffic."*
- Always connects the mechanism to a business outcome (ROI, win rate, pipeline predictability, time saved)

**CTA habits — always soft, always low-friction, always one ask:**
- `Worth a chat?`
- `Open to a quick chat?`
- `Would it not make sense to connect and see if [Company] would be able to get similar value out of 6sense as y'all do with [Competitor]?`
- `Would you be totally opposed to a quick chat in the coming weeks to see how 6sense could help...`
- `Would it make sense to take a look as you get settled?`
- `Are you exploring ways to automate more of your go-to-market?`

**Sign-off:** `Thanks,` or `Best,` — never `Cheers`, never `Warm regards`.

**Never use:**
- `I hope this finds you well`
- `Just wanted to reach out`
- `Circling back` / `Bumping this up`
- `I'd love to`
- Generic templated phrases with placeholder feel
- Heavy corporate jargon (`leverage`, `synergize`, `actionable`, `solutioning`)
- Em-dashes for dramatic effect (use them sparingly, naturally)
